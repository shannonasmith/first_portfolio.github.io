About Me


Since I could barely walk, I always wanted to know how things worked.
The same goes for computers.
But there's a lot more to me than just my love of tech.

I’ve always been an artist. Even at a young age, I was always drawing.
In high school, I decided I was going to try my hand at “Commercial Art.”
In college, that manifested into Industrial Design because I loved to work
with my hands, and I thought that would teach me new mediums and allow me to
utilize my love of drawing. I learned a lot, but I felt that being successful
in that field meant focusing more on designing instead of production.
So I walked away from it and joined the Navy, hoping for a fresh start.

Currently, I'm earning a Master's Degree at Virginia Tech, with a specialization
in Software Development and Cybersecurity Policy.

In my spare time, I draw, paint, sculpt and build. I’ve worked with wood, metal, and
clay, and absolutely love every medium that is out there. I was never able to just
pick one art form and commit to it, against everyone's advice.

I hope you like what I’ve done so far, and I hope you will follow me on my adventure
to see what I might do next!
